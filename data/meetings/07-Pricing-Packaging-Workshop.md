## Discussion notes
### Buyer vs user
We assumed:
- users: operators/analysts who need clarity
- buyers: team leads who care about efficiency and governance

### Packaging proposal
- **Starter:** core search + structured extraction (decisions/actions/risks)
- **Pro:** personalization (sequencing) + eval harness + admin controls
- **Enterprise:** governance (constraint registry UI), audit exports, SSO

### Metering
We rejected metering on “number of decisions improved” (too vague).
We suggested metering on neutral units like “workspaces” or “active seats,” but kept it hypothetical.

### Incentive alignment
We discussed how pricing can create harm:
- pricing per recommendation could encourage spam
- pricing per action taken could encourage automation beyond trust

## Decisions
- Packaging will be aligned to capability maturity: core → personalization → governance.
- We will not meter on recommendation volume (incentive risk).
- Governance features (audit/export/registry) are positioned for Enterprise tier.
- A free demo mode includes a small synthetic dataset for evaluation.

## Risks
- **Mispricing trust:** If pricing encourages automation, product trust erodes.
- **Over-tiering:** Too many tiers confuse buyers.
- **Undervaluing governance:** Buyers may not realize why guardrails matter.

## Key insights (optional)
- Governance is often the “real product” for enterprise AI.
- Pricing shapes behavior; avoid incentives that reward noisy suggestions.
